## What you may do without permission

- Use the Marks in plain text to refer **truthfully** to the project — e.g.

  blog posts, talks, and documentation that discuss the project.
- State that your fork or distribution is **derived from** or **based on**
  POLYROB.

- Naming a **fork, product, service, or company** "POLYROB" / "Selfrule" (or a
  confusingly similar name) in a way that implies it is the official project or

- Using the Marks or logos as **your own** product/app name, icon, domain, or
  social handle.
- Any use that suggests **official status, sponsorship, or endorsement** where
  none exists.

## Forks

You are free to fork the code under the MIT License. If you distribute a
modified version, please **rename it** so users are not misled into thinking it
is the official POLYROB. You may accurately state that your version is "based on
POLYROB".

For trademark permission or questions, contact the maintainers through the
repository (see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) and [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)).

This policy is modeled on common open-source trademark policies (e.g. those of
the Linux and Rust projects) and does not restrict any rights granted to the
source code by the MIT License.
